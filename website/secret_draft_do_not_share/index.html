City of Corvallis • May 19, 2026 Ballot

Vote YES on Measures
2‑143, 2‑144, 2‑145, 2‑146

Four straightforward amendments to modernize Corvallis's 1948 City Charter — unanimously recommended by a nonpartisan task force and unanimously referred by City Council.

Learn About the Measures Donate to the Campaign

The Corvallis City Charter is our community's governing constitution — originally adopted in 1948, last amended in 2021. After months of careful study, the Charter Review Task Force unanimously recommended these four updates. The City Council voted unanimously to put them on your ballot.

Why Vote Yes on All Four

🏛️

Modernizes a 1948 document

Much of the charter dates to 1948. These updates bring language and procedures into the 21st century.

⚖️

Aligns with state law

Removes provisions that duplicate or conflict with Oregon state law, reducing legal risk for the city.

✨

Cleaner, clearer governance

A leaner charter focused on core principles is easier to understand, maintain, and follow.
